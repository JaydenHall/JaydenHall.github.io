Problem Solving

A time I was blocked on a simple problem:

One on the challenges we were working on required us to create a function that took a string( a sentence ) and remove the word buzz from it. Some sentences contained the word multiple times. I worked through and was able to write the function to remove buzz but was stuck on the final test as the word buzz was written with capital letters. I thought maybe I could convert the text to all lower case but that also failed the test. I started by googling how I could check the word buzz without converting it all to lower case. I looked for a while with no luck. I then called my girlfriend to take to her about it( she knows nothing about coding just needed a vent). While talking to her I realised I could use an if statement and that if the word was changed to lower case was not equal to buzz return the word. I found this process surprisingly funny. I felt like I had done the hard part and now I was stuck on something simple I should of been faster to pick up. What I learn’t from this process was a better understanding of built in methods and functions. I had spent the extra time googling than I otherwise would. Also came across arrow functions and I started to read there.

A time I solved a problem in an elegant way:

I’m not sure there is anything particularly elegant about my code at the moment so ill write about when I solved a problem confidently. During the same challenge as above we had to take a string and return it with the first letter of each word changed to a capital. I started the task by writing a list of small steps I needed to achieve and decided that after each step I would use console.log to check if I was on the right path. This was my favourite task as I was able to complete it without getting stuck for to long at each point. It gave me a great deal of confidence as it felt like the hard work was sinking in. What I learnt was to not get to cocky. I came off this task thinking I had cracked it to spend about 3 hours on the following task.

Problem solving techniques:

There have been some techniques I have used more than others and I feel that might be because they are more intuitive. I was quick to turn to google and ask my facilitator for help. After spending more time on the katas, I found that console.log was probably the most important tool I had at this stage and when I was moving nicely it was something I was using often.Pseudocode and the rubber ducky technique are also techniques I’m starting to see value in. I think in the next sprint I will start the minesweeper task by writing pseudocode. Reflection and asking my peers for help are the two I have done less on. I felt like I didn’t want to distract my peers from their study but in reality them helping me would probably help them cement their knowledge.
